How AI is reshaping food distribution

Automated order extraction is quietly removing hours of manual work from wholesalers' days — and changing what the supply chain can do.

For decades, the food supply chain has run on phone calls, faxes, and email threads. Orders arrive in a dozen formats, get re-keyed by hand, and errors compound at every step. GrubMarket's Enterprise AI was built to change that.

From inbox to order, automatically

Automated order extraction reads emails, texts, PDFs, and even voicemails, then turns them into clean, structured orders — no manual entry required. What used to take a sales team hours now happens in seconds.

Why it matters for wholesalers

Fewer errors mean fewer credits, happier customers, and time given back to teams who would rather be building relationships than typing line items. It also creates a clean data trail that powers better forecasting and pricing.

Built for food, not generic

Generic automation tools don't understand produce, perishability, or procurement. GrubMarket's models are trained on the realities of the food supply chain, so they handle the messy, real-world inputs the industry actually runs on.
