Item 1 — Business

GrubMarket, Inc. ("GrubMarket," "the Company," "we," or "our") was founded in 2014 and operates a technology-enabled food supply chain platform spanning three integrated segments: a business-to-business wholesale marketplace, cloud-based enterprise software (including our WholesaleWare ERP, GrubAssist enterprise AI suite, Orders IO mobile commerce, and GrubPay payments products), and direct-to-consumer grocery delivery operated through a family of regional food-hub brands. The Company does business across all 50 U.S. states and in nine countries internationally, with commerce activity touching more than 70 countries.

Since inception, the Company has completed more than 70 acquisitions of regional distributors and software providers, integrating each onto its shared technology platform. The Company believes this acquire-and-integrate strategy, combined with organic software adoption, has been the primary driver of its growth.

Item 1A — Risk Factors

An investment in the Company's securities would involve risks, including but not limited to the following illustrative categories:

  • Perishability and food safety. The Company's business involves the purchase, storage, and distribution of perishable goods, which are subject to spoilage, contamination, and recall risk.
  • Acquisition integration risk. The Company's growth strategy depends materially on identifying, completing, and integrating acquisitions; failure to do so successfully could adversely affect results.
  • Reliance on technology and AI. Disruptions to the Company's software platforms or AI-driven order and inventory systems could impair operations for the Company and its customers.
  • Agricultural and macroeconomic conditions. Weather, climate, and commodity price volatility affecting growers in the Company's network could impact supply and margins.
  • Competition. The Company competes with regional distributors, national foodservice suppliers, and enterprise software providers, some with greater resources.

Item 2 — Properties

The Company's principal executive offices are located in San Francisco, California. The Company leases warehouse, cold storage, and distribution facilities across its operating states, including facilities gained through acquisitions such as Coast Citrus Distributors (McAllen, TX; San Diego, CA; Union City, CA) and Schoenmann Produce (Houston, TX).

Item 7 — Management's Discussion & Analysis

Revenue grew each fiscal year presented, driven by acquisition-related growth and increased software attach rates among wholesale customers. The Company reports it has been profitable on an EBITDA basis for three consecutive fiscal years. In its most recent private financing, the Company raised $50 million in a Series H round at an approximately $4.5 billion pre-money valuation, which management believes reflects continued investor confidence in the Company's AI and eCommerce strategy.
