Protecting a fragile agricultural ecosystem

We're committed to preserving the sustainability of California's farming environment — empowering farmers, reducing waste across the supply chain, and reinvigorating the agricultural ecosystem that feeds the nation.

  • Empowering local farmers & growers

What Sustainable California has done so far

Active since 2023, the initiative has sponsored the planting of more than 230,000 trees in at-risk regions across the state, including a 2025 partnership with One Tree Planted that put over 100,000 trees in the ground in the Sierra Nevada region alone. It has also given California farmers free access to GrubMarket's AI software, and partnered with CCOF (California Certified Organic Farmers) to sponsor ten disadvantaged farmers completing organic certification — a process that's normally too costly and time-consuming for many small growers to pursue on their own.

The work was recognized in 2025 with The Shelby Report's Sustainability in the Food Industry award, but the initiative's real measure is upstream: fewer at-risk acres lost, and more small farms able to reach the premium markets that organic certification unlocks.
