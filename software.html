Built for perishables, not retrofitted from retail

Generic retail ERPs treat produce like packaged goods with fixed SKUs and shelf lives. WholesaleWare was built from day one for the fresh food supply chain, where a case of tomatoes can shrink, ripen, or grade down before it ships. That means lot tracking, catch-weight billing, and grower settlements are core features, not add-ons — which is why GrubMarket calls it the food industry's fastest-growing ERP.

The suite connects three layers that used to be separate systems: WholesaleWare for the back office, Orders IO for customer-facing mobile ordering, and GrubPay for collecting and reconciling payments — all sharing the same data, so a change in one shows up instantly in the others. Farmigo extends the same platform to CSA farms and food hubs managing shares, memberships, and recurring billing.
