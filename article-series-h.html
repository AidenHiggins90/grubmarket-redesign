GrubMarket raises $50M Series H to fuel eCommerce and AI transformation

A $50 million round at a $4.5 billion pre-money valuation will accelerate GrubMarket's AI software and global eCommerce growth across the food supply chain.

Funding · February 2, 2026

GrubMarket, an AI-powered technology company transforming the American food supply chain, announced a $50 million Series H financing round from Future Food Fund, Portfolia Funds, Liberty Street Funds, RD Heritage Group, Flume Ventures, MY Securities, and other participants, valuing the company at $4.5 billion pre-money.

A round built on a year of major milestones

The investment follows significant company achievements. GrubMarket acquired Delta Fresh Produce in April 2025, followed by its largest acquisition — Coast Citrus — in June 2025. The company launched its first-ever Inventory Management AI Agent in July 2025 to automate inventory workflows, and released a Reporting AI Agent in September 2025 for automated business analysis. GrubMarket also acquired Procurant, a SaaS eCommerce platform facilitating $5.5 billion in annual Gross Merchandise Volume for over 850 customers across 14 countries.

Investing from a position of strength

GrubMarket has experienced an incredible acceleration in growth over the last 12 months. This Series H funding will turbocharge our AI software and business growth.Mike Xu, CEO of GrubMarket

GrubMarket operates across all 50 U.S. states and has a global presence in nine countries, conducting business in over 70 countries. The company was named to the CNBC Disruptor 50 list for three consecutive years (2023–2025, ranking No. 20), received "Inventory Management Solution Provider of the Year" in April 2025, and won the 2025 "Sustainability in the Food Industry" award.

Sustainable California

GrubMarket's Sustainable California initiative, active since 2023, has planted hundreds of thousands of trees, supported organic farmer certification, and protected natural habitats. In 2025, the initiative partnered with One Tree Planted to plant over 100,000 trees in the Sierra Nevada region, provided California farmers free access to AI software, and partnered with CCOF to sponsor 10 disadvantaged farmers completing organic certification.
